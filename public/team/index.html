Meet the SIPPS Team

SIPPS is a program developed & run primarily by graduate students, postdocs, and staff in Columbia’s Psychology Department.

Program Founders


Ana DiGiovanni is a PhD student in the Couples Lab. She studies social support processes and interpersonal emotion regulation strategies (i.e., co-rumination) that occur in close relationships (e.g., romantic relationships, friendships), especially during times of stress.

email

Anna Vannucci is a 2nd year PhD student working the DAN Lab. She studies the role that early experiences and affective learning and memory processes play in shaping children’s neuro- development, emotional behaviors, and emergence of psychopathology.

email

Core Team


Paul Alexander Bloom is a 4th-year PhD student in the Tottenham Lab, interested in how early environments impact cognitive and affective development across the lifespan.

email

Amaesha Durazi is a lab manager in the DAN Lab, interested in the socio-emotional development of children and the effects of environmental factors on brain development and behavior.

email



Claudia Espinoza-Heredia is a PhD student working with Dima Amso in the DCN Lab, interested in the role of environmental factors and experience on early brain maturation.

email

Camille Gasser is a PhD student in the Davachi Lab, interested in how our prior knowledge about the world shapes our ability to learn and remember new information.

email



Manasi Jayakumar (she/her) is a PhD student in the Aly Lab, interested in interactions between attention and memory, and the neural mechanisms underlying these interactions.

email

Arlene Lormestoire is a PhD student in the Davachi Lab, interested in how we leverage commonalities across distinct episodic experiences in support of new learning.

email



Ben Silver is a PhD student in the SCAN Lab. He’s interested in how we learn and update our knowledge of the self and others, and how emotion motivates cognition.

email
